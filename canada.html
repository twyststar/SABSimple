Canada

Canada is one of the top international student destinations in the world.

With one of the longest coastlines, vast wilderness, world-class cities, and a culture of tolerance and diversity, Canada is a natural destination for thousands of international students.

One of the biggest reasons students choose to come to Canada is the quality of a Canadian education. A Canadian degree is widely recognized as equal to one from the United States, Australia, or the United Kingdom.

Canada is one of the safest countries in the world, with a low crime rate. In terms of social progress and development, according to Global News, Canada was ranked the second country in the world with the highest quality of life.

In Canada, students can pleasantly combine an enriching academic life with diverse and entertaining leisure activities.

English and French are both official languages in Canada and share equal status, but anywhere you choose to go in Canada, you can easily survive just by speaking and communicating in English.

Gulf Islands Secondary School

The Gulf Islands International Program is truly committed to ethnic diversity and the synergy that results when young people from around the globe work together to create a better world. Given our strong emphasis on intellectual and social development, the Gulf Islands International Program is a natural choice for highly motivated students who value a commitment to academic and personal development.

  • Specialty Programs

Gulf Islands Secondary School boasts a reputation for academic excellence, student support, winning sports teams, outstanding performing and visual arts, and exceptional computer technology programs, including computer animation, robotics, webpage design, computer programming, computer assisted drafting, film and photography.

Qualicum School District

Qualicum School District provides international students from all over the world the opportunity to live with a local family, study in a Canadian school and enjoy the wonderful quality of life we enjoy on Vancouver Island.

Qualicum School District has an abundance of athletic, arts and cultural organizations. There are many opportunities for our children and youth to participate in Music, Dance, Theatre and Fine Arts. Athletes have the opportunity to pursue their passions and excel at many team sports including basketball, soccer, baseball, volleyball, ice hockey, rugby and American football. Individual sports include tennis, golf, swimming, hand ball, skiing, snowboarding, horseback riding, karate, and taekwondo. Our Academy Programs include Golf, Soccer, Music and Hockey.

Ballenas Secondary School is located in Parksville and enrolls 1000 Canadian students and 100 international Students. Kwalikum Secondary School is located in Qualicum Beach and enrolls 750 Canadian students and 75 International Students.

Both schools have a tradition of Academic Excellence with graduates going on to post-secondary institutions in B.C., other parts of Canada and the USA. An International Student Counselor is based at each school to provide academic counseling and assistance in adjusting to Canadian life.

Sooke School District

Victoria’s Sooke School District (No. 62) serves the Greater Victoria Westshore communities of Colwood, Langford, the Highlands and Bear Mountain as well as the seaside community of Sooke. The district is one of the fastest growing school districts in the province.

Sooke Schools offer a comprehensive curriculum which includes academic and elective courses. Math, Chemistry, Physics, Biology, Environmental Studies, English Literature, Social Studies, History, Geography, and a choice of foreign languages including French, Spanish, and Japanese are standard academic courses. Elective courses include courses in Business Education (Accounting, Entrepreneurship, Marketing), Computer Science, Fine Arts (Drama, Musical Theatre, Visual Art, Band), and Applied Skills (Food Studies, Technical Education, Cosmetology). A variety of Advanced Placement (AP) courses are available.

Academy Programs also offer specialized training in Hockey, Soccer, Dance, Rugby, Baseball, and Equine Studies (Horseback Riding). A French Immersion stream of courses is available for students with advanced French language skills.

Delta School District

Located 30 minutes from Vancouver, 20 minutes from the airport, and touching the USA border, Delta has 31 schools in our three towns (Delta, Ladner, and Tsawwassen).Delta staff run and monitor the custodianship and homestay program personally with 24 hour service (we don’t contract homestay care to private businesses). We run monthly sightseeing activities such as ski trips to Whistler, whale watching, camp excursions and more. 

Delta has low ESL populations with 90% English-speaking students in many schools, so international students will learn English quickly in immersion environments. Delta ranks consistently among the top school districts in British Columbia for high graduation rates and outstanding results on government examinations. Delta graduates are accepted in all major universities and colleges around the world.    

Surrounded by water, Delta has beautiful beaches, golf courses, and natural environments. Delta residents are highly educated and have a good standard of living in modern, well-kept homes. Delta boasts the most hours of sunshine with the driest, mildest winters in the Vancouver area.

Rock Mountain School District

Rocky Mountain School District

The Rocky Mountain International Student Program (RMISP) welcomes students from around the world to its public schools in Kimberley, Invermere, Golden, Revelstoke, Cranbrook, and Fernie in beautiful British Columbia, Canada. RMISP manages the international student programs in three BC public school districts: School District No. 5 (Southeast Kootenay), School District No. 6 (Rocky Mountain), and School District No. 19 (Revelstoke). This combined area includes the world-famous Rocky Mountains as well as the skier's-paradise Purcell and Selkirk mountain ranges.

Once you arrive here, you’ll notice there is a lot of space for a relatively small population. You’ll probably also notice how special our location is. All of our schools are located in or near the world-famous Rocky Mountains in British Columbia. That means you’ll be surrounded by mountain peaks, rivers, forests, and lakes and you’ll be able to see waterfalls, glaciers, canyons, and wildlife. It is an amazing place to experience nature and outdoor recreation.  

Take your study-abroad experience to the next level with Rocky Rewards program! Earn points for participation, volunteerism, and good work habits in school. Students who earn enough points are rewarded with a free trip to Vancouver at the end of the school year!

Ontario Schools

Ottawa Carleton School Board

Ottawa, the nation’s capital, is a showcase for Canadian history and culture and a leading centre for education, high technology, life sciences, medical sciences and biotechnology sectors. The reputation of its universities and colleges attracts students from across Canada and from around the world. 

The Ottawa-Carleton District School Board (OCDSB) has been serving students in the capital region of Canada for over one hundred years. We provide international students with the opportunity to choose an educational path that best suits their interests and goals. Students can choose to come to Ottawa to graduate from high school or for a shorter period of study to improve their English and cultural competency.

More Reasons to Study in Ottawa:

  • Friendly, safe and family-oriented community
  • Bilingual community (English and French)
  • Opportunity to attend post-secondary institutions located in the area
  • Many outdoor activities within 15 minutes of downtown (skiing, hiking, canoeing, etc.)
  • World class hospitals including the Ottawa Civic, General and the Children’s Hospital of Eastern Ontario
  • Over 100 embassies and diplomatic missions
  • 29 museums, including 12 exhibiting national treasures
  • Fourth largest metropolitan area in Canada

Newfoundland and Labrador

Newfoundland International Student Education Program (NISEP)

Newfoundland and Labrador is one of the friendliest and safest places in North America. A place where laughter is a recurring theme; a place where its people are recognized for their hospitality, warmth, and love of music and culture. This is where you may look up to examine a high-rise building, pause to smell the roses, all without the shoving and pushing of time-starved city-walkers.

Schools throughout the province offer a curriculum recognized and supported by the Provincial Department of Education including AP and IB selections.

A number of the schools are located in the capital city of St. John's. Today the population of the greater St. John’s area is between 250,000 – 300,000, while the provincial population is 527,000. The metro region includes Mount Pearl (population near 25,000) and Conception Bay South (population near 25,000) both of which are very close to the city of St. John's.

In addition, a number of schools are located in rural communities outside the city. These communities include Corner Brook (population 20,000) Deer Lake (population 5,000), Pasadena (population 3,300), and Stephenville (population 6,700) on the West Coast, Bay Roberts (population 5,400), Carbonear (population 4,700), Placentia (population 3,900), Clarenville (population 33,000) on the Avalon peninsula, and Gander (population 11,000) and Grand Falls (population 14,000) in central Newfoundland.

New Brunswick

New Brunswick French International Student Program

An opportunity to LIVE and STUDY French in francophone schools in New Brunswick, Canada's only officially bilingual province.

Our homestay program welcomes students from grades six through 12, for one semester, sessions of four to 12 weeks, or a full year.

Our schools are located in cities and rural communities throughout the province's three francophone school districts. They offer high quality academic programs in a safe environment aimed at helping participants to learn or improve French language skills.

Participants are carefully matched with a francophone host family, with whom they can experience first-hand, the warm hospitality and rich cultural history of the Acadians. This is a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity. It includes fascinating activities for every season. By the end of your stay, you will be able to converse comfortably in French, and will have made lasting friendships.
