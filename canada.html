Canada

Canada is one of the top international student destinations in the world.

With one of the longest coastlines, vast wilderness, world-class cities, and a culture of tolerance and diversity, Canada is a natural destination for thousands of international students.

One of the biggest reasons students choose to come to Canada is the quality of a Canadian education. A Canadian degree is widely recognized as equal to one from the United States, Australia, or the United Kingdom.

Canada is one of the safest countries in the world, with a low crime rate. In terms of social progress and development, according to Global News, Canada was ranked the second country in the world with the highest quality of life.

In Canada, students can pleasantly combine an enriching academic life with diverse and entertaining leisure activities.

English and French are both official languages in Canada and share equal status, but anywhere you choose to go in Canada, you can easily survive just by speaking and communicating in English.

Sooke School District

Victoria’s Sooke School District (No. 62) serves the Greater Victoria Westshore communities of Colwood, Langford, the Highlands and Bear Mountain as well as the seaside community of Sooke. The district is one of the fastest growing school districts in the province.

Sooke Schools offer a comprehensive curriculum which includes academic and elective courses. Math, Chemistry, Physics, Biology, Environmental Studies, English Literature, Social Studies, History, Geography, and a choice of foreign languages including French, Spanish, and Japanese are standard academic courses. Elective courses include courses in Business Education (Accounting, Entrepreneurship, Marketing), Computer Science, Fine Arts (Drama, Musical Theatre, Visual Art, Band), and Applied Skills (Food Studies, Technical Education, Cosmetology). A variety of Advanced Placement (AP) courses are available.

Academy Programs also offer specialized training in Hockey, Soccer, Dance, Rugby, Baseball, and Equine Studies (Horseback Riding). A French Immersion stream of courses is available for students with advanced French language skills.

Burnaby School District

Located in the centre of Greater Vancouver, Burnaby School District offers top quality INTERNATIONAL EDUCATION opportunities for students from overseas to study in one of its 8 secondary schools while experiencing beautiful BRITISH COLUMBIA, CANADA!

With superior academic programs and modern facilities, all located within a city of vibrant green spaces, modern shopping malls, safe residential neighbourhoods, and state of the art public transit, choosing an education in Burnaby is the right choice.

Burnaby School District’s EXPERIENCE PROGRAMS offer a focus on learning Canadian culture, improving language skills, and providing a wide range of exciting elective courses for 5-month and 10-month terms!  

Burnaby School District’s ACADEMIC PROGRAMS offer a wide variety of excellent academic, advanced placement, honours, visual and performing arts, technology, computer studies and sports courses!

International students at the secondary level (ages 13 to 18 years) have the opportunity to study alongside Canadian students in any of our 8 secondary (grades 8 to 12) schools. Students are integrated into regular courses and, where necessary, are provided with added English Language Learning (ELL classes). Burnaby School District has a long tradition of exceptional academic excellence.

Newfoundland and Labrador

Newfoundland International Student Education Program (NISEP)

Newfoundland and Labrador is one of the friendliest and safest places in North America. A place where laughter is a recurring theme; a place where its people are recognized for their hospitality, warmth, and love of music and culture. New Brunswick

New Brunswick French International Student Program

An opportunity to LIVE and STUDY French in francophone schools in New Brunswick, Canada's only officially bilingual province.

Our homestay program welcomes students from grades six through 12, for one semester, sessions of four to 12 weeks, or a full year.

Our schools are located in cities and rural communities throughout the province's three francophone school districts. They offer high quality academic programs in a safe environment aimed at helping participants to learn or improve French language skills.

Participants are carefully matched with a francophone host family, with whom they can experience first-hand, the warm hospitality and rich cultural history of the Acadians. This is a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity. It includes fascinating activities for every season. By the end of your stay, you will be able to converse comfortably in French, and will have made lasting friendships.
